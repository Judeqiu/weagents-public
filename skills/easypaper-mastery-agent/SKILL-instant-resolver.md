# Instant Resolver Skill

## Overview

**24/7 emergency help for stuck questions—never stay stuck for more than 5 minutes.**

The Instant Resolver is your "panic button." When you're staring at a question and have no idea how to proceed, upload a photo and get:
1. Immediate step-by-step solution
2. Explanation of the underlying concept
3. Links to similar practice questions
4. Automatic topic tracking (adds to Weakness Hunter if recurring)

**Core principle:** Getting stuck is normal. Staying stuck kills momentum. This skill gets you moving again.

**Available:** 24/7, responds within minutes

## When to Use

**Use this skill when:**
- You've attempted a question for 10+ minutes and made no progress
- You don't understand what the question is asking
- You got an answer but it doesn't match the mark scheme
- You're reviewing a paper and can't follow the mark scheme
- You're doing homework and hit a roadblock

**Trigger phrase:** *"I'm stuck on this"* + photo upload

**Do NOT use for:**
- Questions you haven't attempted (try first!)
- Concept learning from scratch (use textbooks first)
- Full paper walkthroughs (use mark schemes)

📋 QUESTION ANALYSIS:
   Topic: Circular Motion
   Concept: Vertical circular motion with tension
   Difficulty: Standard A-Level (9702)

📝 STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION:

Step 1: Identify forces at the top of the circle
   - Weight (mg) acts downward
   - Tension (T) acts downward (toward center)
   - Both contribute to centripetal force

Step 2: Apply Newton's 2nd Law toward center
   F_net = ma_c
   T + mg = mv²/r

Step 3: Solve for tension
   T = mv²/r - mg
   T = m(v²/r - g)

Step 4: Calculate minimum speed (when T = 0)
   0 = mv²/r - mg
   v² = gr
   v_min = √(gr)

📚 KEY CONCEPT EXPLAINED:
   At the top of vertical circular motion, BOTH tension
   and weight point toward the center. Students often
   forget tension also points down (toward center), not up.

   Remember: Centripetal force is ALWAYS toward center.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## Common Question Types & Resolver Approach

### Type 1: "I have no idea where to start"

**Resolver response:**
```
🎯 STARTING STRATEGY:

1. Identify the TOPIC from key words:
   - "circular path" → Circular motion
   - "rate of change" → Calculus/differentiation
   - "equilibrium" → Forces/moments

2. List GIVEN information:
   - m = 0.5 kg
   - v = 4 m/s
   - r = 0.8 m

3. Identify what you NEED to find:
   - Tension T = ?

4. Recall relevant EQUATION:
   - For circular motion: F = mv²/r

5. Connect given → equation → answer
```

### Type 2: "My answer is wrong but I don't know why"

**Resolver response:**
```
🔍 ERROR ANALYSIS:

Your working:     T = mv²/r = 0.5 × 4² / 0.8 = 10 N
Correct answer:   T = 5 N

❌ Your error: Forgetting weight
   You calculated the NET centripetal force correctly,
   but tension alone doesn't provide all of it.

✅ Correct approach:
   At top: T + mg = mv²/r
   T = mv²/r - mg
   T = 10 - 5 = 5 N

💡 LEARNING: Always draw force diagram first!
   Common oversight in vertical circular motion.
```

### Type 3: "I don't understand the mark scheme"

**Resolver response:**
```
📖 MARK SCHEME BREAKDOWN:

Mark scheme says:
   B1: States momentum conserved ✓ (you got this)
   M1: Attempts mv equation ✓ (you got this)
   A1: Correct masses substituted ✓ (you got this)
   A0: Final answer incorrect ❌ (you lost this mark)

Why you lost the mark:
   - You calculated speed, but mark scheme wanted velocity
   - Direction matters! You needed + or - sign

💡 KEY INSIGHT: Read the question carefully—does it ask
   for speed (scalar) or velocity (vector)?
```
